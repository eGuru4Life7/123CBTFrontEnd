Problem Solving

Module

Khushi Muhammad's eldest son Munir, who was studying in the city, was on holidays and was trying to help his father with the program. He was stressed due to a lot of problems in his life. He found the information on problem-solving useful to get help with his tuition fees.



↓

List of problems:

  • 1- No job
  • 2- Low self-esteem
  • 3- No money for exam fee
  • 4- Cannot speak in public
  • 5- Problem with studies
  • 6- Shyness


→

He chose to solve the problem number 3, as it was doable, and was likely to have a significant impact on his well-being

↓

List of solutions:

  • Talk to the father.
  • Talk to local businesspeople or some organisation.
  • Borrow money from someone
  • Talking to social welfare department to find out if there is a local NGO.
  • Talk to teacher for exemption in fee
  • Talk to friends
→

Choosing a solution: He examined all the solutions, one by one. Finally, he chose to speak to the teacher, based on these advantages and disadvantages

↓
Advantages Disadvantages
Don't have to pay back Will have to talk to the teacher
I have nothing to lose, so there is no problem to talk He is known for being rough
The government announced recently, fee concession for deserving students The teacher will insult
I will be able to concentrate on studies People will make fun of me if they find out
Don't have to worry about job till I finish exams




↓

Steps in applying the solution:

  • Getting ready for the meeting
  • Finding more about fee exemption
  • Writing down what I have to say in meetings
  • Role play and rehearsal as a practice of meeting
  • Talking to a friend who can accompany
  • Talking to the teacher for a meeting
  • Getting there in time
