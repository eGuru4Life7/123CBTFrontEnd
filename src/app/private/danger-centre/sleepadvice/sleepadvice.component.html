Healthy sleep environment consists of the following:

  • Comfortable bed
  • Temperature of the room (neither too hot nor too cold)
  • Noise free environment
  • A room without excessive light

Tips for improving sleep:

Cooling down time: Your brain needs some time to switch off. Some people find reading in bed helpful others might see that reading keep them awake. Try to find out what helps you.

Physical problems: Physical problems can cause sleeplessness. Physical discomfort or pain can cause sleep disturbance.

Sleep routine: Keeping a sleep routine is crucial. Going to bed and leaving it at regular times can be helpful for many people.

Psychological problems: Sleep problems resolve once depression gets treated. Simple anxiety management techniques like breathing exercise etc. (mentioned earlier) can be very helpful.

Tea, coffee and nicotine: Drinking tea or coffee before going to bed can make people feel alerted. Not drinking tea or coffee after 6 PM can help many people. Similarly, cutting down on cigarettes before going to bed can also be helpful.

Day Naps: Day naps can lead to disturbed sleep. In fact, it has been suggested that sleeping in the morning hours might be linked to depression.
