Unhealthy patterns of communication

Passive people

Passiveness, otherwise known as submissiveness is opposite to aggression. The passive people usually don't express their feelings. They don't tell others what they want. They are unable to express their anger and bottle up their feelings. While passiveness is different to being shy or quiet, shy or quiet individuals are often passive. Passive individuals avoid conflict by remaining quiet as they avoid expressing their point of view. A failure to express their point of view occurs outside of conflict – it is frequent in conversations and social interactions. When they are asked what they’d like, where they want to go, or what they want to do, they passively respond: “I’m happy with whatever you want”. In fact, they are never happy.
